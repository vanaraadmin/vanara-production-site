01 Your Island Guide

Vanara recommends

There is no right way to experience Koh Chang. Only your own rhythm.

Whether you prefer quiet beaches, scenic drives, rainforest hikes or authentic fishing villages, the island rewards those who slow down.

Everything on this page has been selected by our team to help you discover places we genuinely love and regularly recommend to our guests.

03

West Coast

Beaches that made Koh Chang famous.

01

Lonely Beach

Relaxed atmosphere, cafés and sunsets.

Once known as Koh Chang's backpacker paradise, Lonely Beach has evolved into one of the island's most enjoyable places for cafés, restaurants, beach bars and relaxed afternoons.

02

Kai Bae

One of the island's iconic viewpoints.

Although the beach itself is beautiful, the famous viewpoint overlooking the offshore islands is one of the most photographed locations on Koh Chang.

03

Bang Bao

Fishing village, lighthouse and boats.

Wooden walkways stretch above the sea, connecting seafood restaurants, dive shops and longtail boats heading towards Koh Rang Marine Park.

04

East Coast

A quieter side of Koh Chang. Authentic, peaceful and beautifully untouched.

04

Long Beach

One of the island's most remote beaches.

Reaching Long Beach takes time, but the reward is one of Koh Chang's quietest stretches of sand. Outside high season you may find yourself almost completely alone.

05

Wai Chek Beach

Wild nature, almost no development.

Surrounded by dense jungle, Wai Chek is one of Koh Chang's hidden gems and remains largely untouched by tourism.

06

East Coast Road

The island's most authentic drive.

Small villages, coconut plantations, local cafés and sea views accompany one of the most enjoyable scooter rides on Koh Chang.

05

National Park

Inside the rainforest. The green heart of Koh Chang.

Tropical Rainforest

Ancient jungle, giant trees and wildlife.

More than seventy percent of Koh Chang is protected National Park. Walking trails reveal enormous trees, tropical birds, butterflies, hornbills and occasionally monkeys.

Waterfalls

Cool freshwater hidden beneath the canopy.

Several waterfalls are accessible across the island, each offering refreshing pools during the rainy season and peaceful forest walks throughout the year.

06

Scenic Roads

Sometimes the journey is the destination.

Koh Chang's mountain roads connect every side of the island with dramatic viewpoints, tropical jungle and constant changes of scenery. Riding slowly is the best way to appreciate them.

07

Salakphet

Paddle through the mangroves. One of Koh Chang's most peaceful experiences.

Mangrove Forest

Silence, birds and mirror-like water.

Salakphet is home to Koh Chang's extensive mangrove forest. Kayaking through its narrow waterways offers a completely different perspective on the island, far removed from the beaches and busy roads.

08

Boat Trips

The sea beyond Koh Chang. Snorkelling, islands and turquoise water.

Daily excursions depart from Bang Bao, taking visitors to Koh Rang Marine Park and nearby islands where crystal-clear water and coral reefs offer some of the region's best snorkelling.

Temples

Quiet places of reflection.

Buddhist temples remain part of everyday island life. Visitors are welcome to explore respectfully, appreciating both the architecture and the peaceful atmosphere.

09

Every Evening

End every day facing west.

From Lonely Beach to Kai Bae, Koh Chang's western coastline offers unforgettable sunsets almost every evening during the dry season.

10 Island Culture

Local Life

Koh Chang isn't only about where you go. It's about the people you meet.

Small roadside cafés, family restaurants, fruit stalls, fishermen repairing their boats, children returning from school, monks collecting alms at sunrise.

These everyday moments are what give Koh Chang its genuine character. Slow down, smile often and let the island reveal itself naturally.
